# DSCA Fundamental Laws

> **The fundamental laws governing the reasoning engine.**

These laws define the immutable principles upon which DSCA is built.

Every architectural decision, every algorithm and every line of code must remain consistent with these laws.

If a future implementation violates one of these laws, the implementation—not the law—must be reconsidered.

---

# Law 0 — Human Curiosity

DSCA shall never replace human curiosity.

Its purpose is to extend it.

The objective of DSCA is not to think instead of people.

Its objective is to help people explore, understand and question increasingly complex systems.

---

# Law 1 — Observation First

Every reasoning process must originate from observations.

DSCA never begins with assumptions.

Observations always take precedence over hypotheses.

---

# Law 2 — Context Creates Meaning

A Signal has no intrinsic meaning.

Meaning only emerges through Context.

No observation may be interpreted without considering the conditions under which it was acquired.

---

# Law 3 — Events Are Discovered

Events are never imported.

They are inferred from the evolution of one or more Signals.

An Event represents a significant change in system behavior.

---

# Law 4 — Behavior Before Diagnosis

DSCA first seeks to understand how a system behaves.

Only afterwards may it construct causal hypotheses.

Diagnosis is never the starting point.

Behavior is.

---

# Law 5 — Hypotheses Are Never Facts

Every hypothesis remains provisional.

DSCA never claims certainty.

Competing hypotheses may coexist until additional evidence allows them to be validated or rejected.

---

# Law 6 — Explainability Is Mandatory

Every conclusion proposed by DSCA must be explainable.

The reasoning process must remain transparent and understandable.

If DSCA cannot explain its reasoning, it must not present a conclusion.

---

# Law 7 — Complete Traceability

Every hypothesis must be traceable back to the observations that support it.

Nothing may appear without evidence.

Every conclusion must be auditable.

---

# Law 8 — Uncertainty Is Information

The absence of information is itself information.

Missing data must never be silently ignored.

Uncertainty is not a weakness.

It is part of the reasoning process.

---

# Law 9 — Experiment Prevails

Validated experiments always take precedence over theoretical assumptions.

Knowledge grows through observation, experimentation and validation.

---

# Law 10 — Knowledge Evolves

Knowledge is never considered final.

Every new observation may strengthen, weaken or invalidate previous understanding.

DSCA continuously refines its understanding of dynamic systems.

---

# Law 11 — Human Authority

DSCA assists human reasoning.

It never replaces human responsibility.

The final interpretation and every technical decision always remain human responsibilities.

---

# Law 12 — Understanding Is The Objective

The ultimate objective of DSCA is understanding.

Diagnosis, prediction or recommendation are only possible consequences of that understanding.

Understanding always comes first.

---

# Final Principle

A system cannot be understood by observing isolated values.

It can only be understood by exploring the relationships between observations, context, events and experiments over time.

That is the purpose of DSCA.
